Holistic Support

Programs Designed by Women, for Women

Every program we run is shaped by the priorities of LBQ women in Homabay County. We take an integrated approach — recognising that health, safety, legal protection, and economic security are inseparable.

⚕️

Health & Wellness

We provide accessible, confidential, and LBQ-affirming healthcare services — from sexual reproductive health education to mental health peer counselling and referral networks. Our health team partners with local clinics to train providers in culturally sensitive care.

Key activities include free health camps, psychosocial support groups, mental health first aid training, and awareness campaigns on SRHR rights.

Mental Health SRHR Peer Counselling Health Camps
⚖️

Legal Aid & Advocacy

Our legal team provides direct legal assistance to LBQ women facing discrimination, violence, or violations of their human rights. We also conduct policy advocacy at the county and national level to push for legislative change.

We have supported over 150 cases including employment discrimination, housing eviction, family violence, and access to justice matters.

Legal Assistance Policy Advocacy Human Rights Court Support
💼

Economic Empowerment

Financial independence is a cornerstone of safety and dignity. Our economic empowerment program delivers vocational training, business skills workshops, savings group facilitation, and microfinance linkages for LBQ women.

We have helped over 400 women start or grow small businesses, with a focus on sustainable, locally-rooted enterprise.

Business Training Microfinance Savings Groups Vocational Skills
📚

Education & Awareness

Changing hearts and minds requires sustained community education. We run sensitisation programs in schools, religious institutions, and community spaces — creating dialogue around LBQ rights and reducing stigma.

Our community educators are trained LBQ women who can speak authentically to their experiences and build bridges with allies.

School Programs Community Sensitisation Ally Building Media Campaigns
🛡️

Safety & Protection

LBQ women facing violence, displacement, or housing insecurity receive emergency support through our protection program. We maintain a network of safe houses and provide crisis counselling, legal referrals, and relocation support.

We also run a 24/7 emergency hotline for women in crisis situations who need immediate assistance.

Safe Housing Crisis Intervention Emergency Hotline Relocation Support
🤝

Community Building

Isolation is one of the greatest challenges facing LBQ women in Homabay. Our community building program creates regular safe spaces — both physical and digital — where women can connect, share, heal, and organise together.

Monthly social events, annual retreats, and an active online community ensure that no LBQ woman in Homabay has to navigate life alone.

Safe Spaces Support Groups Social Events Online Community
