Our Story

Born from Community, Built for Community

The Homabay LBQ Network was established in 2018 by a group of courageous women who recognised the urgent need for a dedicated organisation serving Lesbian, Bisexual, and Queer women in Homabay County.

In a region where structural discrimination, limited healthcare access, and economic marginalisation intersect, we provide holistic support grounded in the lived experiences of LBQ women. Everything we do is designed by and with the communities we serve.

Today we are a growing network of advocates, healthcare workers, legal professionals, and community organisers united by a single belief: every woman deserves dignity, safety, and the freedom to live authentically.

Our Purpose

Mission, Vision & Goal

01

Our Mission

To advance the rights, health, and dignity of Lesbian, Bisexual, and Queer women in Homabay County through community-led advocacy, accessible services, and solidarity-building.

02

Our Vision

A Homabay County where LBQ women live freely, safely, and with full access to the rights and resources they deserve — without fear of discrimination or violence.

03

Our Values

Solidarity, dignity, intersectionality, community ownership, and radical compassion guide every decision we make and every program we deliver.

04

Our Approach

We centre the voices of LBQ women in all program design, implementation, and evaluation — ensuring that solutions emerge from lived experience, not assumptions.
